Praised for her “coppery” and “richly coloured” tone, British mezzo-soprano Phoebe Rayner is a recent graduate of the Royal College of Music and Royal Northern College of Music. Last year, she was selected as a Stoller Hall Emerging Artist and Britten Pears Young Artist specialising in contemporary music and the development of new works.

Recent operatic highlights include Flora in La Traviata (Barefoot Opera), Alcandro (cover) in L’Olimpiade (Vache Baroque), Nerone in Agrippina (Hampstead Garden Opera), La chatte/L'écureuil in L’enfant et les sortilèges (RCM Opera Studio), Concepción in L'heure Espagnole (Ernest Reed Symphonic Orchestra), Stewardess in Flight (directed by Jeremy Sams, RCM Opera Studio), Jacinta/Lieutenant II in Rip Van Winkle (Gothic Opera), Hänsel in Hänsel und Gretel (Westminster Opera Company), and a Spring Opera Showcase (directed by Peter Relton, Clonter Opera).

On the concert platform, she has recently performed Miss Donnithorne's Maggot (The Stoller Hall, Manchester), Pribaoutki (Britten Pears Contemporary Ensemble, Snape Maltings), and a solo recital as part of the Manchester Song Festival 2024. A passionate song performer, Phoebe was a member of the RCM’s SongPlus scheme, with whom she performed recitals for International Women’s Day and an American song collaboration with the Juilliard School.
